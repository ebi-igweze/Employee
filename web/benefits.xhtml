Holiday / Vacation Policy

  • Personal Holiday
  • Religious Holiday
  • Vacation Period
In addition to scheduled paid holidays, eligible employees are given two floating holidays annually to be used as personal time off. Before scheduling a personal holiday, the employee must obtain approval. Requests for personal holidays must be made in writing not less than 10 days in advance of the requested date.
Cyberspace recognizes that there may be religious holidays (other than those already designated at holidays) that employees would like to observe. It may be possible to arrange these holidays as scheduled days off, authorized absences without pay or personal time off. Requests for time off to observe religious holidays must be approved.

At the end of the employee's first year as a full-time employee of CyberSpace, an employee is entitled to 9 days of paid vacation. The employee's vacation days increase to 15 days after five years of continuous employment with CyberSpace, 25 days after 15 years of continuous employment with CyberSpace, and 35 days after 30 years of continuous employment with Cyberspace.

Vacation time may be taken in increments of one full day but in all cases must be pre-scheduled and pre-approved. One day of vacation for every five days that an employee is entitled to may be carried over to the following year, but must be used before 10th of January.

Cyberspace does not provide paid vacation time for part-time employees.

Pregnancy and Maternity Leave policy

To be eligible for a pregnancy leave of absence, the employee must be a nontemporary, full-time female employee.

Maximum Length of Leave

The maximum length of pregnancy leave allowed is 9 weeks. If the employee needs a longer leave due to medical complications, the employee should notify Cyberspace as soon as possible. The additional leave will be treated the same as any other medical or disability leave.

Written Requests

A written request for pregnancy leave must be submitted within a reasonable time. The employee must submit a written doctor's statement, indicating the anticipated delivery date. The employee should inform Cyberspace of the expected duration of her pregnancy leave so that Cyberspace may plan around the absence efficiently until her return.

Transfers

An employee requesting pregnancy leave may also ask for a transfer to another less strenuous or less hazardous position if so desired. The request must be in writing and must state the reason for the transfer.
